Open Secret (2018) Art by Andrea Bowers & Case Study by Emma Worthen

Introduction

Describing the Case Study Subject

This case study will be highlighting Open Secret (2018) by Andrea Bowers. This piece of art is an installation created using archival pigment prints. Open Secret (2018) discusses international movements against sexual harassment and assault, specifically the #MeToo and Times Up movements that became viral in 2017. The piece features individuals who faced accusations, their response to the accusations placed upon them, and other information regarding the accusations.1

Visual Description

Visual Properties of the Art Installation

Open Secret (2018) by Andrea Bowers primarily features text in a variety of fonts with the addition of images that depict individuals who faced accusations of sexual harassment. The piece appears quite stationary as these textual elements and imagery are shown on top of backgrounds of solid color.

The Open Secret (2018) display includes two wall installations, the walls measuring at 13 x 62 x 1.6 feet, and the images featured in the piece measure at a width of 24 inches with varying lengths.2 The display also provides several office chairs that are placed around the installation. The installation covers the entirety of the two walls used and the piece is comprised of approximately 200 archival pigment prints.

Each print lists the name and occupation of an accused person, followed by their response and a summary including details of the accusations, the job/career status of the accused, legal actions, and other important details, sometimes accompanied by related images.3 The predominant color used in Open Secret (2018) is red. The prints exist multiple shades of red while textual elements are either white or black. Some images are in black and white while others are in color. The prints included appear to almost exist in strips, starting at the top of the installation wall and draping their way to the bottom. Some even stretch onto the ground beneath. All of the text used in these prints is placed in a horizontal fashion, reading from left to right. While the prints themselves follow vertical lines down the installation walls, the textual content within them follow horizontal lines. The images used are often placed between sections of text or at the top of a print.

The installation walls are placed in the center of the room, leaving quite a large amount of open space on all sides of the walls. This allows the piece to feel much more three-dimensional (3D), despite consisting of two-dimensional (2D) prints. The entire piece utilizes straight and sharp lines, rather than anything curved. As the installation walls stand at a height of 13 feet, viewers are much shorter than the display. However, text is featured from top to bottom in the prints which would incite viewers to look both up and down.

Context

Social and Political Context

Open Secret (2018) by Andrea Bowers documents the development of #MeToo and Times Up, the international movements against sexual harassment and assault.1

What began as the me too. movement was founded by survivor and activist, Tarana Burke, with the vision of providing resources, support, and pathways to healing to those who have been impacted by sexual violence. In 2017, the #MeToo hashtag went viral and woke up the world to the severity of the problem that is sexual violence. There were millions of people from all walks of life saying, me too. and sharing their stories.4

The Times Up movement was started in 2018 by a group of over 300 women in Hollywood, including high-profile celebrities such as Reese Witherspoon, Natalie Portman, and Shonda Rhimes. The organizations goal was to create change that would lead to safety and equity in the workplace.5 This goal of change included several avenues, from creating legislation to combat sexual misconduct to ensuring women of color and the LGBTQ+ community are represented equally.6

These two movements go hand-in-hand as they share a similar vision for womens empowerment. The Times Up movement can be thought of as a solution-based, action-oriented next step in the #MeToo movement.5

Visual Analysis

How the Art Interacts with its Context

Open Secret (2018) by Andrea Bowers interacts with the context of the #MeToo and Times Up movements as it showcases instances of the problem at hand, sexual violence, and what was done in response. It showcases these instances through the text and imagery included in the collection of archival pigment prints. The piece includes details of the accusations made against people in relation to sexual violence, the accused persons response, and any legal action that was taken. It tells stories of sexual violence that show why the discussion surrounding sexual violence is an important one to have.

To be more specific, the textual content of the installation includes the occupation of the accused person, tying into how the Times Up movement placed focus on sexual violence and inequality in the workplace. The presence of the provided office chairs that sit around the installation walls are also representative of this workplace focus. They are not only placed there for practicality, allowing viewers to use them when reading the texts, but also to reference the nature of sexual harassment as a workplace violation.3

The text and images organized in the archival pigment prints read as something similar to a news article. News articles often highlight important news and draw the attention of large groups of people. Having the content of this piece set up in such a way is perhaps intended to communicate the importance of the topic at hand, as well as communicate that it is an issue that impacts many people.

Additionally, the color red is very prevalent in this installation, helping represent the urgency of the topic and the discussion surrounding it.

Historical Comparison #1

Comparing to Past Art

Open Secret (2018) by Andrea Bowers is similar in form to some of Antonio Manuels work with flans during the military dictatorship in Brazil. Just as Manuel primarily used text and images in these pieces, Andrea Bowers has utilized text and images through archival pigment prints. We can see Manuels use of text and imagery showcased in pieces like Flan: Movimento Estudantil (1968), which translates to, Student Movement. We draw another similarity between the two artists and their work as they are both discussing social or political movements of some kind. While Antonio Manuel is highlighting the Student Movement that rose while Brazil was under military dictatorship, Andrea Bowers is highlighting movements against sexual violence like the #MeToo and Times Up movements.

Historical Comparison #2

Comparing to Past Art

While they are very different in form, Open Secret (2018) by Andrea Bowers and Rape of the Sabine Women (1579-1583) by Giambologna are similar in ways. Their contrasting forms, archival pigment print and marble sculpture, make them very visually different from each other but they share a common theme. This theme is sexual violence. While Open Secret (2018) discusses sexual violence through text, Rape of the Sabine Women (1579-1583) depicts violence through the physical appearance of the sculpture. Sexual violence was often used to represent dynamics of power in early modern Europe so while acts of sexual violence itself may not have been the intended focus at the time, the topic is still present. Depictions or discussions of sexual violence through art have changed over time, as seen through these two pieces, but they both contain them.
